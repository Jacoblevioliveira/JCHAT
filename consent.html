Study Overview

You are invited to participate in a research study that investigates Generative AI use and determine your level of trust in the generated response output.

Generative AI platforms (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, etc...) have gained prominence in everyday use and is slowly replacing traditional online web search (Mulligan, 2025). In 2023, one in ten web users asked generative AI first for their online search (The Verge, 2023). 68% of generative AI platform users use this tool to seek answers to their questions.

During this 60-minute study, you will be placed to sit in front of a desktop computer within a study room. For each study trial, you will be asked to type in pre-assigned prompts onto the locally-hosted AI chat interface, which is not connected to external generative AI models. The AI will display results in different output speeds. After each trial, an 8-item trust questionnaire will ask you about your perceived trust in the Generative AI output results.

Benefits to Future Research and the General Public

The information obtained from this research will lead to a better understanding of how generative AI use is influenced by user trust. Specifically, this study will help clarify how different output characteristics, such as the speed of the response, affect a user's perceived level of trust in the AI's answer.

These findings will contribute to the growing body of knowledge on human-AI interaction and the psychology of trust. This information may have future implications for designers and developers, helping them to create AI chat interfaces that are perceived as more reliable, thoughtful, and helpful, ultimately improving the experience for the general public.
