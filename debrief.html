Dear Participant,

We appreciate your participation in our study “AI Chat Output and Trust”. Thank you for spending the time helping us with our research! This study examines whether the "flowing type output" AI chat text response output can elicit increased levels of trust when people seek answers from generative AI platforms.

During the experiment, we asked you to type specific prompts into our AI chat interface. As a study control, every study participant received the same pre-scripted AI chat response output in the mock interface. We only manipulated the speed in which the prompt was delivered on the screen. After each result, we asked you to rate the trustworthiness of the results using the TAI.

We previously told you that our AI chat interface was live and was local hosted in our computer servers. We could not give our participants the complete information about providing same pre-scripted AI responses on a mock AI chat interface because it would have influenced participants’ behaviour during the study that would have affected the survey results. We wanted to elicit a more authentic and ecologically valid response with the AI chat interface from our participants.

We hope you understand the need for deception in this study. Most studies in psychology research do not involve deception in their designs.

If participating in this study (e.g., answering questions about your mood) has raised any personal issues or concerns for you, Campus Wellness at the University of Waterloo has many services available to support student wellness. Their services are listed on their website at the following URL: https://uwaterloo.ca/campus-wellness/ or by phone at 519-888-4096.

Confidentiality

This study is being conducted by researchers in the Department of Psychology at the University of Waterloo, strictly for research purposes. The association between your identity and your data in this study is confidential. Your data will only be associated only with your Participant ID number. The information and consent letter provided to you will be stored for at least 7 years in a locked filing cabinet in PAS 2247, 2249, 2251, 2256. Electronic data will be stored for at least 7 years on a secure, password protected computer protected by the departmental server accessible only to authorized researchers. All of the data will be summarized, and no individual can or will be identified from these summarized results.

De-identified data related to your participation may be submitted to an open access repository or journal (i.e., the data may be publicly available). These data will be completely deidentified/anonymized prior to submission by removing all personally identifying information (i.e., names, identification numbers, birthdays, email addresses) before submission and will be presented in aggregate form in publications. This process is integral to the research process as it allows other researchers to verify results and avoid duplicating research. Other individuals may access these data by accessing the open access repository. Although the dataset without identifiers may be shared publicly, your identity will always remain confidential.
